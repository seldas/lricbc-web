**3/8/26 Pastor’s Word**
“No, I don’t really believe in retirement”

Reginald Tsang

“So why did you have three?”... It’s a joke. If I can retire so many times, they’re not really retirements. Ultimately, I think we could retire the term “retirement” anyway.

It’s definitely not in the Book… A lot of times, people think that what we practice conventionally in the West, is probably prescribed somewhere in the Good Book, so that’s why retirement is such a common practice.

Sorry, it’s not in the Book and it’s really a relatively recent invention. It’s certainly not recorded as a recommended way of life, or even a concept, anywhere in the best book.

Mr. Bismarck’s social politics... The first recorded Western pension plan for retirement was set in Germany by her Chancellor, Otto Von Bismarck, in 1881. It was a political social insurance decision (insurance means $$ to support “retirement") to fight off his opposition forces of radical socialism, who wanted even more social guarantees. Bismarck set the pension-retirement age at 70. He himself did not retire then, and was forced to resign just before age 75. (1)

Photo 1: The “iron man” responsible for starting Western pensions (social insurance $$$) for retirements, was himself forced to resign at age 75. (2)

A famous refusal to retire… What happens if people are in very meaningful work among those with lots of needs, and they feel impassioned about continuing their work? That sounds like a wonderful spirit, and we wish we had more spirits like that!

My heroes and mentors, Dr. Paul Brand and his wife Dr. Margaret Brand, served for decades in India as medical missionaries, and became world-renowned for their outstanding work among leprosy patients. But what most impressed me was Paul’s mother.

Granny Brand @ 95

So she left the agency and went on her own back to India to continue to serve the great needs there. She died at the age of 95, surrounded by the mountain tribal people who loved her, and who were essentially her family till the end of her days.

Photo 2: Granny Brand non-retired by age 95

My father never officially retired… Dad was a general practice physician who worked until he was sick and could not work anymore. Whereupon “we” [actually, our teenage son, see (3)] brought him over to America, where he passed away a year later.

Dad barely took any vacations in his life, and he lived a very fruitful and meaningful life looking after his patients and his family. Many relatives told us that he was so generous with his help when they were in distressed situations, especially when they were living in places with lots of political problems.

Respect from young and old… Dad was indeed a man well respected by young and old. In his last year, even though he was now living in the middle of USA in Cincinnati, friends and relatives from Asia would drop by to see him because they were so grateful for his life’s work.

Young people always remembered he had a needle in his hand, in the days when practitioners often treated everything with injections! Remembrances can be so funny.

Photo 3: Non-retiring Dad trying to inspire me from babyhood

My father-in-law never retired either… He practiced medicine in a small town in Southern Thailand until he passed away. He felt a tremendous responsibility for his patients, so much so that he never ever took official vacation.

He never even took vacation to visit his elder daughter in Bangkok, Thailand, nor to visit the younger one (my wife) in Hong Kong and the USA. We never heard him say a word about retirement, which was also indeed my dad’s attitude.

Photo 4: Father- and mother-in-law, non-retiring, and source of strength for the community, inspiring especially their youngest daughter

praise—the fruit of lips that openly profess his name.”

“不，我真的不相信退休”

曾振锚

“那你为什么有三次？”... 这是个笑话。如果我能退休这么多次，那就不是真正的退休了。最终，我认为我们无论如何都可以让“退休”一词退休了。

它绝对不在那本书（the Book，特指《圣经》）里……很多时候，人们认为我们在西方按惯例所实行的做法，大概是在那本“好书”里的某个地方被规定了，这就是为什么退休成了一种如此普遍的做法。

抱歉，它不在那本书里，而且它确实是一个相对较新的发明。它当然没有在那本最好的书里被记载为一种被推荐的生活方式，甚至连一个概念都算不上。

俾斯麦先生的社会政治……西方有记录的第一个退休养老金计划，是由德国的宰相奥托·冯·俾斯麦（Otto Von Bismarck）在1881年设立的。这是一项政治性的社会保险决定（保险意味着用$$来支持“退休”），目的是对抗他的激进社会主义反对势力——那些人想要更多的社会保障。俾斯麦把养老金退休年龄设定为70岁。他自己当时并没有退休，而是在75岁之前被迫辞职。（1）

照片1：负责启动西方退休养老金（社会保险 $$$）的“铁人”本人被迫在 75 岁时辞职。

一次著名的拒绝退休……如果有人在一项非常有意义的工作中，服事那些有许多需要的人，而且他们对继续这项工作充满热情，那会怎样？那听起来是一种美好的精神，我们也真希望有更多这样的人！

我的英雄和导师，保罗·布兰德博士和他的妻子玛格丽特·布兰德(Paul and Margaret Brand)博士，在印度作为医疗传教士服侍了几十年，并因其在麻风病患者中的杰出工作而享誉世界。但最让我印象深刻的是保罗的母亲。

95岁的格兰妮 · 布兰德 奶奶（Granny Brand）

……保罗·布兰德博士的母亲本人也是一位到印度的传教士。她的派遣机构命令她在 70 岁时退休。她对该命令提出上诉，但被拒绝。

因此，她离开了该机构，独自回到印度，继续满足那里的巨大需求。她去世时享年 95 岁，身边都是爱她的山区部落人民，直到她生命的尽头，他们基本上就是她的家人。

### 图片2：格兰妮·布兰德(“Granny Brand”)到 95 岁时未退休。

我父亲从未正式退休... 爸爸是一名全科医生，他一直工作到生病，不能再工作了。因此，“我们” [实际上是我们十几岁的儿子，见 （3）] 把他带到了美国，一年后他在那里去世了。

爸爸一生中几乎没有休过任何假，他过着非常充实和有意义的生活，照顾他的病人和他的家人。许多亲戚告诉我们，当他们处于困境时，尤其是当他们生活在有很多政治问题的地方时，他非常慷慨地提供帮助。

来自老少的尊敬……爸爸确实是一个深受老少尊敬的人。在他生命的最后一年，尽管住在美国中部的辛辛那提，来自亚洲的朋友和亲戚仍会特地来看他，因为他们对他一生的工作心怀感激。

在那个医生常常“什么病都打针”的年代，年轻人总记得他手里总是拿着一根针！回忆真是有趣。

### 图片3：不退休的爸爸试图从小就激励我

我的岳父也从未退休……他在泰国南部一个小镇行医，直到去世。他对病人怀有极大的责任感，以至于从未正式休过假。

他甚至从未度假去泰国曼谷看望他的大女儿，也从未去香港和美国看望小女儿（我的妻子）。我们从来没有听他说过一句关于退休的事，这也是我爸爸的态度。
