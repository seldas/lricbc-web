A CONTEXTUALIZED EMBODIMENT OF THE CROSS:[15]

JOHN SUNG’S LIFE AND MINISTRY

Introduction

“Lord! This body of mine belongs to God.

The learning, fame, and wealth of the world are all vain;

only the merit of the cross endures forever.

Thank You, Lord! Use my hands to work for You, and let them be nailed to the cross for You.”[16]

After three years of rural evangelistic ministry in China in 1930, John Sung offered this praise to God. Echoing the Apostle Paul’s confession in 1 Corinthians 2:2, Sung’s entire life was characterized by the exaltation of Jesus Christ and Him crucified.[17] In view of the abundance of historical materials concerning his life and ministry, this chapter adopts the “cross” as its central motif and approaches the subject from the perspective of contextualization. It presents a chronological account of his life journey and ministerial development, thereby providing the historical and contextual background for the subsequent analysis of his contextualized preaching.

John Sung was born on September 27, 1901 (the 15th day of the 8th lunar month in the 27th year of the Guangxu reign of the Qing Dynasty), at 4:00 p.m., in Fengji Village, Putian County, Xinghua Prefecture, Fujian Province.[18] During a field visit at Caohu Church on Nanri Island in Fujian[19] on June 8, 2025, Minister Huang Yuan[20] introduced the history of Caohu as the birthplace of the [21]gospel in Putian, emphasizing that it was this land of Putian that nurtured Sung.[22] Before introducing Sung, the name of his spiritual predecessor, Lin Zhenzhen, must be mentioned.

Lin Zhenzhen (1823–1877, Chinese: 林振珍) was the first Christian of the Methodist Episcopal Church and the first local pastor in the Xinghua (modern-day Putian) region. He is regarded as the pioneer and founder of the gospel work in that area.[23]  In 1863, he encountered the gospel in Fuzhou and was converted, experiencing a radical transformation in his life; he was delivered from being an “opium ghost”[24] into a gospel messenger. He immediately returned to his hometown, Nanri Caohu, to preach the gospel. Not only did he lead his entire family to Christ, but he also gradually established a family-centered local church network. He was proficient in Putian dialect, Minnan dialect, and Fuzhou dialect, and organized an evangelism team whose ministry extended to Quanzhou, Yongchun, Putian, Fuzhou, and Fuqing. He is regarded as the founder of the Fuqing Church.

In contrast to the early Western missionaries who labored in tears for many years with relatively limited fruit, the indigenous ministry led by Lin Zhenzhen bore abundant fruit in a remarkably short time.[25] By 1871, the number of believers in the Xinghua region had reached 774. In pastoral ministry, he emphasized both the spiritual life of believers and the solid grounding of doctrine, while actively promoting indigenized mission work, training local preachers, and establishing a self-supporting, self-governing, and self-propagating church structure.[26] Although he endured severe persecution and suffering in his ministry, he remained steadfast in his conviction of “laying down one’s life for the sake of the gospel,” and continued to expand gospel work. The gospel foundation and contextualized practice established by Lin Zhenzhen opened the historical beginning of the church in the Putian region and laid the groundwork for later revival movements. Moreover, the so-called “twenty firsts” in the history of the Putian church—such as the first newspaper, school, hospital, and leprosy hospital—underscore its significant contributions to society and its role as fertile soil for revival.[27] Therefore, it may be rightly said: “Without Lin Zhenzhen’s pioneering gospel ministry and the seeds of revival in Putian, as well as the gospel-rich soil of Putian itself, the later revival movements led by John Sung around the world would not have been possible.”[28]

十字架的处境化表达[1]

——宋尚节的生命与事工

引言

“主阿！我這身體是神的身體。

世界的學問、名譽、富貴，都是空虛，

只有十字架的功勞永遠長存。

感謝主！以我的手為祢工作，為祢釘在十字架上”。[2]

在中国乡村三年的布道事工之后（1930年），宋尚节向神献上了这样的赞美。使徒保罗说：“因为我曾定了主意，在你们中间不知道别的，只知道耶稣基督并他钉十字架”（哥林多前书2:2）。与保罗相似，宋尚节的一生最显著的特点之一就是高举耶稣基督并祂钉十字架。[3] 鉴于关于他生命与事工的历史资料十分丰富，本章以“十字架”为中心主题，并从“处境化”的角度展开叙述。本文按时间顺序呈现他的生命历程与事工发展，从而为后续关于其处境化讲道的分析提供历史与背景基础。

宋尚节于1901年9月27日（清光绪二十七年农历八月十五日）下午四时，出生于福建省兴化府莆田县凤迹村。[4] 在2025年6月8日对福建南日岛草湖教会[5]的实地考察中，黄媛传道[6]介绍草湖为莆田福音的发源地，并强调正是这片土地孕育了宋尚节。[7] 在介绍宋尚节之前，必须先提及一位属灵前辈林振珍。

林振珍（1823–1877，中文：林振珍）是卫理公会在兴化（今莆田）地区的第一位基督徒，也是当地第一位本地牧者，被视为该地区福音工作的开拓者。[8] 1863年，他在福州接触福音并归信基督，生命经历了巨大转变，从“鸦片鬼”[9] 成为福音的使者。他随即回到家乡南日草湖传扬福音，不仅带领全家归主，也逐渐建立以家庭为核心的本地教会网络。他精通莆田话、闽南话与福州话，并组织布道团队，其事工扩展至泉州、永春、莆田、福州及福清等地。他被视为福清教会的奠基者。与早期西方宣教士多年劳苦而果效有限[10]相比，林振珍所推动的本地化事工在较短时间内结出丰盛果实。[11] 到1871年，兴化地区信徒人数已达774人。在牧养方面，他强调信徒属灵生命与稳固教义根基，并积极推动本地宣教事工，训练本地传道人，建立自养、自立、自传的教会结构。[12]尽管在事工中经历严重逼迫与苦难，他仍坚定持守“为福音舍命”的信念，持续扩展福音工作。他所奠定的福音基础与处境化实践，开启了莆田地区教会历史的起点，并为后来的复兴运动奠定了基础。

此外，所谓莆田教会历史中的“二十个第一”，如第一份报纸、第一所学校、第一所医院及麻风病院等，也显示其对社会的重要贡献，并使该地成为复兴的沃土。[13] 因此，可以合理地说：“若没有林振珍的开拓性福音事工与莆田复兴的种子，也没有莆田这片福音深厚的土地，就不会有后来宋尚节在世界各地所带来的复兴运动。”[14]
