## A Contextualized Embodiment of the Cross: John Sung's Life and Ministry

### Part 4: Confinement at Bloomingdale Hospital

*(continued from previous issue)*

The Chinese philosopher Mencius said: "Therefore, when Heaven is about to confer a great responsibility upon a person, it first tests his will through suffering, exhausts his body through labor, starves his flesh, subjects him to poverty, and frustrates his undertakings. In this way, it strengthens his mind and character, increasing his capacity for what he is unable to do."

Almost every servant whom God has greatly used must go through trials and refinement. Joseph was put in prison; Moses underwent forty years of refinement in the wilderness; David was pursued by Saul for more than ten years, learning to trust in God during a long period of exile and waiting. John Sung's experience in a New York mental hospital also became an important period in his life, preparing him for his later evangelistic ministry.

After Sung was born again, his life underwent a significant transformation. His fervent spiritual pursuit, frequent public testimonies, and direct criticism of liberal theology caused some faculty members at Union Theological Seminary (UTS) in New York to feel uneasy. In *My Testimony*, he wrote:

> "I often wept and earnestly persuaded people to come to Christ and enjoy the peace of forgiveness He gives. Driven by my fervent love for the Lord, I sincerely pointed out the sins of some preachers and pastors. I asked them, in love, to kneel with me in prayer, asking the Lord's blood to forgive us for the sins of not faithfully proclaiming the truth, or for neglecting and being lazy in the work of the ministry. Unfortunately, very few were willing to humbly accept such loving admonition, and even fewer actually corrected themselves."

He further said that he loved singing hymns, and after being born again he became even more fervent — sometimes singing loudly, sometimes softly chanting, sometimes weeping while praising the Lord, sometimes laughing while thanking God. The seminary therefore concluded that he had developed mental illness. Regarding whether he truly had psychological issues, scholars have different views. However, it can be affirmed that this period played an important role in guiding and shaping his life.

On the morning of February 17, 1927, Sung bought a new Bible. On his way back, he met three children, each of whom wrote the same word on the road: *"Rest."* Soon afterward, he was sent to a mental hospital by the institution. This experience became an inner confirmation: God was leading him into a season of "rest" in his spiritual life. He even wrote in his diary: *"The word is Rest! I was well asleep. In reality, I did not sleep all night."* — a tension between the spiritual call to rest and the physical inability to sleep.

The next day, when the doctor examined his mental condition by telling him a short story and asking him to repeat it, Sung reproduced it word for word without omission, astonishing the doctor with his remarkable memory.

At first, Sung thought he would only stay for thirty-three days. However, he remained for **193 days**, until August 30, 1927, when he was released with the assistance of Reverend and Mrs. Wilbur Fowler.

During this long stay, he lived in relative isolation and entered into an intensive period of Bible reading — reading through the entire Bible approximately forty times. He also began to meditate on and organize the Scriptures as a whole, a stage later regarded as the important precursor to his method of biblical interpretation and preaching.

Yet this experience was not peaceful. He went through moments of pain, confusion, and even despair. On one occasion, he escaped while the attendant dozed off — jumping from a balcony, running two to three miles, and hiding in a wheat field — only to be discovered by a dog and brought back like a captured fugitive.

On another night, in loneliness and homesickness, he fell into deep despair and contemplated suicide. In that moment, he suddenly heard a voice from above:

> **"My child! You are purchased by My precious blood; how can you take your own life so casually?"**

He replied: *"Lord, I am so humble and insignificant. There is no day when I see the light of the world. I wish to serve You but find no way. Life is worse than death."*

The Lord's voice continued:

> **"All things work together for good to those who love God. If you can endure, after these 193 days of suffering, you will know how to take up the cross and follow Me on the path of Calvary obedience."**

Suddenly, the darkness before his eyes disappeared, and the glory of the Lord shone all around him.

In the hospital, he also came into contact with the painful conditions of other patients — their cries, confusion, and disorderly behaviors enabling him to deeply recognize the brokenness within the human soul. These scenes intensified his burden for souls, and a sense of urgent calling arose: a longing to bring people back to God. This profound burden became one of the defining characteristics of his later ministry.

His wife, Mrs. Sung, later recalled:

> "From that time (his new birth), he sought to understand the truth of the Bible. He loved the Scriptures deeply and was almost never without his Bible. Through reading the Bible, he was most deeply moved, and his love for souls became extremely urgent, like a blazing fire; yet this was mistakenly regarded as madness."

From a spiritual perspective, Wang Tiansheng observed:

> "In the New York mental hospital, after God had broken his pride and self-righteousness one by one, his life was completely transformed: he no longer lived for himself, but for Jesus Christ; he no longer sought to please man or himself, but solely to please Jesus Christ."

## 十字架的处境化表达——宋尚节的生命与事工

### 第四部分：在神病院的日子

*（接上文）*

中国哲学家孟子说："故天将降大任于是人也，必先苦其心志，劳其筋骨，饿其体肤，空乏其身，行拂乱其所为，所以动心忍性，增益其所不能。"

几乎每个被神重用的仆人，都要经历试炼与磨练。约瑟曾被下在监中；摩西经历四十年旷野的熬炼；大卫被扫罗追杀约十多年，在长期的逃亡与等候中学习倚靠神。宋尚节在纽约精神病院的经历，也成为他生命中重要的一段过程，为日后的布道事工作了预备。

宋尚节重生得救之后，生命发生了显著的转变。他火热的属灵追求、频繁的公开见证，以及对自由派神学的直接批评，使当时纽约协和神学院（UTS）的部分同工深感不安。他在《我的见证》中提到：

> "我常流泪苦劝人们来就基督，得享祂所赐赦罪的平安。我更为热忱爱主的心所驱策，很诚恳地指出一些传道人牧师们的罪，我用爱心请求他们和我跪着祷告，求主的宝血赦免我们不忠实传达真理的罪，或是疏忽圣工、怠惰作工的罪。可惜他们很少人肯谦卑地虚心地领受由爱心所发的忠言，而去实行改过的更是寥若晨星。"

他进一步说，他喜欢唱诗歌，在重生之后更加狂歌不已，或时而高歌，时而低吟，时而轻唱，时而流泪赞美主，时而欢笑感谢神。神学院因此断定他已经染上精神病。对于他是否真的存在精神方面的问题，学者们有不同看法，这里不作进一步讨论。但可以肯定的是，这段时间在他生命中具有重要的引导与塑造意义。

1927年2月17日清晨，宋尚节买了一本新圣经。在回程的路上，他分别遇见三个小朋友，他们各自在路上写着同一个字："Rest"（安息）。随后，他被校方安排送往精神病院。路上的经历成为他内心的印证：上帝要他在灵性里进入"安息"的阶段。他甚至在日记中写道："The word is Rest！我终夜不睡"——灵里明白要休息，肉体却无法入睡，这其中体现出深刻的张力。

第二天，当医生再次检查他的思想状态，讲述一则简短故事要求他复述时，他一字不差地写出，令医生对他惊人的记忆力感到意外。

起初，宋尚节以为自己只会在这里停留三十三天便可出院。然而，他实际在医院中停留了**一百九十三天**，直到1927年8月30日，在威尔伯·福勒牧师夫妇的协助下离开医院。

在医院的漫长岁月里，他经历了相对隔离的生活，也进入了密集的读经阶段。在较短时间内，他多次通读整本圣经，据其见证约达四十遍之多。这一阶段后来被视为他属灵释经与讲道方式的重要雏形。

然而，这段经历并非外在意义上的平静安稳。他也曾经历痛苦、混乱，甚至绝望的时刻。有一次，他趁看护人员打盹之际逃离医院，跳下凉台，跑出二三里外，藏身在一片麦田之中。然而最终，他的藏身处被一只西洋狗发现，仍被像捉强盗一样押解回医院。

另一天晚上，在孤独与思乡的情绪中，他陷入极深的绝望，产生自杀的念头。在那绝望之际，他忽然听见上面来的声音：

> **"小子！你是我用宝血所买赎的，怎能随意轻生？"**

他回答说："主啊，卑微的我，生无见天日的一日，欲图报而无从，生不如死，所以自杀。"

主的声音继续对他说：

> **"万事互相效力，叫爱上帝的人得益处，你若能忍耐，过了这一百九十三天的苦难，你就知道怎样背上十字架跟我走各各他顺服之路了。"**

忽然眼前的一团黑暗不见了，主的荣光四面照着他。

在医院中，他不仅经历了个人层面的挣扎，也接触到其他病人的痛苦状态。那些病人的呼喊、混乱与失序的行为，使他深刻体认人类内在深处的破碎与捆绑。这些景象深深震撼他，也进一步加重了他对灵魂的负担，渴望将人带回到神面前。

对于这段经历，其妻宋锦华师母后来回忆说：

> "从那时（重生）起，他就追求明白圣经的真理，他笃爱圣经，几乎手不释卷。由读经受感最深，因而爱人灵魂之心甚是迫切，如同烈火炽燃；而竟被误认为疯狂。"

从灵性的角度，王天声指出：

> "在纽约精神病院里，神把他的骄傲、自义一一地破碎之后，他的人生完全地反转：他不再为自己而活，而是为耶稣基督而活；他不再讨人的喜悦、自己的喜悦，而是单单要讨耶稣基督的喜悦。"
