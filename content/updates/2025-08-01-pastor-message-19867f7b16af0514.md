**8/3/25 Pastor’s Word**
The Baptism of My Nephew

— Reflections on My Trip Back to China, Part IV

---
**There is a unique emotional weight in baptizing one’s own family member. It not only carries the joy of angels rejoicing over a sinner who repents but also bears witness to the fountain of the Lord’s love flowing within the deep bonds of kinship. On June 2nd, our entire family had dinner with the local pastor and coworkers of our hometown church. During the meal, Pastor Wang suddenly suggested, “Why don’t we let Pastor Li baptize Shulun this time?”**
I joyfully replied, “Of course! But we should still ask for Shulun’s own opinion.”

---
**Shulun gladly agreed. This was exactly the shared desire between him and his mother. He hesitated to bring it up himself, but God brought it forth through Pastor Wang’s mouth.**
Shulun is my nephew, the son of my older sister. She had been married for many years without children and had experienced several miscarriages. In our rural hometown, where outdated thinking still lingered, she endured harsh and critical words from her in-laws. She wept and prayed to God, asking Him to give her a child. The Lord heard her prayer, and she gave birth to Shulun. Even while he was still in the womb, my sister prayed that God would grant him wisdom, intelligence, and a heart that feared the Lord. And indeed, God poured out His grace. Shulun grew up to be upright, diligent in his studies, and gifted with both intellectual and emotional intelligence. More precious still, he developed a deep love and understanding for God’s Word.

He also has a remarkable spirit of gratitude. Once, he used a scholarship to buy himself a new bicycle, but it was stolen one evening. My sister was so upset that she couldn’t sleep the entire night. But Shulun comforted her, saying, “Our hearts of gratitude are still too small! When I used my scholarship to buy you a refrigerator, a washing machine, and a massage chair, you never stayed up all night from happiness.” His words were deeply convicting. We are often like Eve in the Garden—failing to give thanks for the abundance of fruit from so many trees and instead grumbling over the one we cannot have.

---
**As a Baptist pastor, I will prefer to do baptism in immersion. Then came the question of where to hold the baptism. Should we go to a hotel pool, a nearby river, or a believer’s home? Pastor Wang spent an entire day making calls but to no avail. Just then, Brother Mu, the husband of Sister Zhao Guiqin—who was once the founder in our hometown church—came to visit.[1] He said, “We have a bathtub at our house. You can use that.” It seemed to be a provision already prepared by God. Sister Zhao had dearly loved Shulun during her lifetime, faithfully praying for his studies and spiritual life every day. Holding the baptism in her home would surely be a beautiful comfort to her in heaven. I truly believe she rejoiced with us that day, just as Revelation 14:13 declares: “The Spirit says, ‘Yes,’ they will rest from their labor, for their deeds will follow them.”**
On June 4th, we held a warm and intimate baptism service at her house, attended by family and a few brothers and sisters in Christ. Standing in that small room, I was overwhelmed by emotion—for it was the very place where I preached my first sermon more than 30 years ago. Back then, we were just a small group meeting around the brick-bed in a village home. As new believers, we always sang before we prayed: “But you will receive the power when the Holy Spirit comes upon you...” That little band of rural believers, once looked down upon by the world, had now been led by the Spirit to testify for the gospel in cities like Qiqihar, Harbin, Beijing, Shanghai, and even as far away as the United States.

In the message, I emphasized: “Baptism is our act of following Christ, obeying Him, and bearing witness to His resurrection. It is also our spiritual union with the Lord—putting off the old self and living out the new. Baptism is not a graduation ceremony, but a commencement.” I encouraged Shulun with lyrics from my own graduation at Nanjing Theological Seminary: “Faith can move mountains and seas; faith can overcome a thousand barriers.” May he, like Daniel and Joseph, trust God wholeheartedly wherever he is and whatever he faces. May he prosper in his work and flourish in life, spreading the fragrance of Christ wherever he goes.

Though it was just a simple bathtub, to us it was the most fitting “baptismal pool.” Compared with the spacious and solemn settings in American churches, this humble vessel helped us better grasp the essence of baptism—not in its outward form, but in its spiritual reality. May the Lord personally guide and preserve Shulun all his life, walking in the path that pleases Him, until that day when he meets the Lord face to face.

8/3/25 牧者之言

外甥的浸礼

——回中国之旅随感之四

为自己的亲人举行浸礼有一份独特的情感。它不仅包含着天使为罪人悔改的欢呼，也承载着主爱的泉源在血浓于水的亲情中涌流的见证。6月2日我们全家与家乡教会牧者和同工一起用晚餐。在用餐时，家乡教会的王牧师忽然提起：“这次让李牧师给书伦受洗吧吧！”

我开心地说：“好啊。不过还是要征求他本人的意见”。书伦欢欣然答应了。这正是他和他妈妈共同的愿望。他没有好意思开口，神却借着王牧师的口提了出来。

书仑是我外甥，我姐姐的儿子。当初姐姐结婚多年一直没有孩子，并且经历过几次流产。在当时思想比较落后的农村，她曾受来自婆家的冷言冷语。她流泪向上帝祷告，祈求主赐予她孩子。主垂听了她的祷告，使她生下了书仑。书仑在母腹中时，姐姐就为他向上帝祈求智慧、聪明和敬畏的心。主果然厚赐恩典，使书仑为人正直，学习努力，智商情商都很高。更难得的是，他对上帝的话语有着深切的热爱和领悟。

他也特别懂得感恩。有一次，他用奖学金买的新自行车被偷走了。姐姐气得一宿未眠。书仑却劝他妈妈说：“我们感恩的心还是太小了！我得奖学金给你买冰箱、洗衣机、按摩椅时，你从来没有高兴得睡不着觉过啊”。他这番话真是令人警醒。我们常像始祖夏娃，不为园中百树之果感恩，反倒因无法得到的那一棵树的果子而怨怼。

我作为浸信会的牧师，自然要作浸水礼，即全身浸入水中。接下来是寻找施洗的地方。该去哪儿？到宾馆的游泳池里、到河边或者到某个信徒家？王牧师忙着联系了一天也没有结果。正在这时，前家乡教会负责人赵桂芹二姐[1]的丈夫牟弟兄来访。他说：“我们家有个浴盆，可以到我们家”。这似乎是神早已安排好的。二姐生前就对书仑钟爱有加，她每天都为他的学业和灵性祷告。书仑的浸礼在她家举行是对她天上最美的安慰。深信她在天堂定会与我们一同欢喜庆祝。正如启示录14:13所说：“圣灵说，是的，他们息了自己的劳苦，作工的果效也随着他们。”

6月4日，我们在二姐家里举行了一个温馨的浸礼礼拜，有家人和几位弟兄姐妹参加。站在那间小屋中，我百感交集——这里正是30多年前我人生第一次讲道的地方。那时的我们，是村里炕头聚会的一小群人。刚信主时，我们总是每次祷告前就唱：“但圣灵降临在你们身上，你们就必得着能力……”那一群被人轻看的农村信徒，在圣灵的带领下，如今已有人走向齐齐哈尔、哈尔滨、北京、上海，甚至远赴美国，作主福音的见证人。

我在分享中强调：“浸礼是我们效法基督、顺服基督、作主复活见证的行动；更是在灵里与主联合，脱去旧人，活出新人。浸礼不是毕业典礼，而是开学典礼。”我用当年在南京神学院毕业典礼上的歌词勉励书仑：“信心能使山海改，信心能渡万重关。”愿他如同但以理和约瑟，无论在何处、遇何境，都全心倚靠神，事业兴盛、生命丰富，在各处散发基督的馨香之气。

虽然那只是一个浴盆，但对我们来说却是最合适的“浸礼池”。相比美国教会宽敞庄严的设施，这简朴的工具反倒让人更深体会——浸礼的意义不在于外在形式，而在于属灵实质。愿主亲自引导、保守书仑的一生，走在祂所喜悦的道路中，直到见主面那日。
