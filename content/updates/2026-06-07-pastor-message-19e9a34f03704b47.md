### A CONTEXTUALIZED EMBODIMENT OF THE CROSS

JOHN SUNG’S LIFE AND MINISTRY

(continued from above)

2. Encounter with a Young Girl Evangelist

During his studies at Union Theological Seminary in New York, the liberal theological atmosphere challenged John Sung’s original foundation of faith and, for a time, led him away from the core of the gospel. At that time, the seminary president, Henry Sloane Coffin (1877–1954), was a leading representative of mainline liberal theology. His theological orientation was shaped by biblical higher criticism and the social gospel movement, emphasizing the ethical and social dimensions of Christianity while relatively downplaying the supernatural and traditional doctrinal core. This theological tendency was precisely what later scholars such as J. Gresham Machen would strongly critique in Christianity and Liberalism (1923).

However, in the midst of this situation, Sung’s life trajectory took a turn before Christmas in 1926. He attended a gospel meeting at Calvary Baptist Church in Midtown Manhattan, New York, where he heard a 14-year-old girl evangelist, Uldine Mabelle Utley (1912–1995), preach.

This event was also reported in the contemporary media. The New York Times published an article describing the impact of this 14-year-old girl’s preaching at Calvary Baptist Church. The then senior pastor, John Roach Straton (1875–1929), introduced her as “a sunny-hearted child anointed with God’s oil.” She had previously preached for six consecutive Sundays at the Cadle Tabernacle in Indianapolis, drawing crowds of more than 15,000 people. According to the report, in the Calvary Baptist Church meeting, she preached the message of sin, salvation, and the cross of Christ in a steady and powerful manner, rather than relying on emotional appeal or rhetorical technique, leading the congregation into a deeply focused spiritual atmosphere. Her two-hour sermon deeply moved the audience, resulting in widespread tears and repentance. The preaching process was described as having a “gradually rising emotional intensity,” in which the congregation moved from quiet listening to deep engagement, culminating in visible emotional and spiritual response at the peak of the message. Those in attendance included not only ordinary believers but also prominent social figures and church leaders. After the message, many came forward in response to the invitation, some weeping and others praying, displaying a widespread response of repentance and spiritual awakening. This event thus became a widely reported gospel gathering in contemporary religious journalism.

Sung later described her as being dressed entirely in white, which from a distance resembled traditional Chinese mourning garments, yet at the same time conveyed a sense of solemnity and reverence. Her voice was clear and resonant, sounding like a warning bell of salvation throughout the congregation. The message she preached was not abstract theological speculation, but a clear and exalted proclamation of the cross of Christ. Sung later recorded: “She set forth the way of salvation without omission, so fully and powerfully that anyone who heard it could immediately grasp the essential core of the Christian faith.”

Sung attended her evangelistic preaching five times and said to his fellow students: “I truly feel that she is more qualified than any seminary president or professor. At the very least, our president should first humble himself and learn from the truth she proclaims before being qualified to hold such a position.”

From this experience onward, Sung gradually formed the conviction that “any preacher who has not received the baptism of the Holy Spirit is not qualified for ministry.” He also deeply longed to enter into the kind of spiritual experience Utley had, yet he could not find a way to attain it.

From a broader historical perspective, this revival-like scene may also be seen as a recurrence of the revival Sung experienced at the age of nine in Xinghua. As a Chinese proverb says, “One cannot see the true face of Mount Lu because one is within the mountain.” After passing through seasons of theological confusion and struggle, Sung came to realize, under the illumination of the Holy Spirit, that his childhood encounter with the Lord had in fact been a precious spiritual treasure.

——宋尚节的生命与事工

（接上文）

遇见一位少女布道家

在纽约协和神学院（Union Theological Seminary）求学期间，自由派神学的氛围对宋尚节原有的信仰根基构成挑战，并在一段时间里使他偏离了福音的核心。当时的神学院院长Henry Sloane Coffin（1877–1954）是主流自由派神学的重要代表人物，其神学深受圣经高等批判学与社会福音运动影响，强调基督教的伦理与社会维度，而相对淡化超自然与传统教义的核心内容。这一神学取向，也正是后来如约翰·加尔文·马臣（John Gresham Machen）在《基督教与自由主义》（Christianity and Liberalism）（1923）中所严肃批判的对象。

然而，就在这样的处境中，宋尚节的生命轨迹在1926年圣诞节前发生转折。他在纽约曼哈顿中城的加略山浸信会教会（Calvary Baptist Church）参加了一场福音聚会，在那里听见一位年仅14岁的少女布道家乌德琳·马贝尔·尤特丽（Uldine Mabelle Utley，1912–1995）传讲信息。

这一事件在当时媒体中亦有报道。《纽约时报》曾刊载关于这位14岁少女在加略山浸信会讲道的影响。当时主任牧师约翰·罗奇·斯特拉顿（John Roach Straton，1875–1929）介绍她为“a sunny-hearted child anointed with God’s oil”（一位心中充满阳光、被神恩膏的孩子）。她曾在印第安纳波利斯Cadle Tabernacle连续六个主日讲道，吸引超过15,000人聚集。据报道，在加略山教会的讲道中，她以稳定而有力的方式宣讲罪、救恩与基督十字架的信息，而非依赖情绪或修辞技巧，使会众逐步进入高度专注的属灵氛围。她长达两小时的讲道，使会众普遍深受触动，出现流泪与悔改的回应。她的讲道过程呈现出一种“不断上升的情感张力”，会众由安静聆听逐渐进入高度投入，并在信息高峰处出现明显的心灵震动与泪水回应。听众中不仅有普通信徒，也包括社会名流与教会领袖，他们在信息结束后纷纷上前回应呼召，有人流泪，有人祷告，呈现出广泛的悔改与属灵回应，使该次聚会成为当时报刊持续报道的显著福音事件。

宋尚节后来描述，这位少女全身穿着白衣，从远处看似中国传统的孝服，却同时带有庄重与敬畏的气质。她的声音清晰洪亮，如同救恩的警钟在会众中回响。她所传的信息并非抽象的神学讨论，而是清楚、高举基督十字架的福音宣告。宋尚节后来记载说：“她将救恩的道路毫无遗漏地陈明出来，既完整又有力，使任何听见的人都能立刻抓住耶稣基督信仰的核心。”

宋尚节一共五次前去聆听她的福音布道，并对同学说：“我真的觉得她比任何神学院的院长或教授更有资格。至少，我们的院长应当先谦卑下来，从她所传讲的真理中学习，然后才配担任院长。”从这次经历开始，宋尚节逐渐形成一个观点：“凡没有领受圣灵洗礼的传道人，都不配进入事奉”。他也深切渴望进入尤特丽所经历的属灵经验，却始终未能找到途径。

从更广阔的历史视角来看，这次复兴性的场景，在某种程度上也可被视为宋尚节九岁时在兴化所经历复兴的再现。正如中国古诗所说：“不识庐山真面目，只缘身在此山中。”在经历信仰的挑战与混乱之后，宋尚节在圣灵光照之下重新认识到，他童年时期与主的相遇，原来是一份极其宝贵的属灵产业。（未完待续）
