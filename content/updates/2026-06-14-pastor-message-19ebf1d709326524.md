### A CONTEXTUALIZED EMBODIMENT OF THE CROSS

JOHN SUNG’S LIFE AND MINISTRY

(continued from above)

3. The Born-Again Experience

In the final night of 1926, John Sung entered a profoundly deep spiritual crisis. His inner world was filled with intense struggle and an existential despair of self-understanding. In prayer, he heard what he understood as God’s warning: “I will destroy the wisdom of the wise” (1 Cor. 1:19). This word struck him like a heavy hammer, shaking the very foundation of his confidence in human knowledge, achievement, and the meaning of life itself.

As the Chinese Lunar New Year of 1927 approached, this inner burden did not lessen but instead grew increasingly heavy. By February 10, 1927, the first day of the Lunar New Year, he could no longer endure this overwhelming spiritual oppression. In this state of crisis, he experienced an intense inner struggle between the Holy Spirit and evil, together with a profound awareness of his own sinfulness and complete helplessness.

That night, in what he later understood as a moment of illumination by the Holy Spirit, his entire life seemed to be laid bare before God. He testified that all his sins—from childhood to adulthood—were replayed before his eyes like a film. In great shame and despair, he took a Bible that had long been untouched, lying at the bottom of his bookshelf, and began to read Luke chapter 23.

In The Testimony of Dr. John Sung, he described this experience as follows:

“Regarding the suffering of the Lord Jesus for my sins, I felt as though I were out of my body, my spirit following Jesus as He carried the cross toward Golgotha. The road was desolate and silent. This vivid and agonizing scene pierced my heart. I too, like a condemned sinner going to be crucified, lowered my head, bent my back, and dared not look at others, but only stumbled along in the footsteps of the Lord. Ah, my friends, you cannot know the anguish I felt in that moment! As I followed the Lord on the way to Golgotha, I felt that the burden I was carrying was beyond what I could bear; the weight of my sins nearly crushed me to death. Suddenly, Jesus was already lifted upon the wooden cross. His head was bowed to one side, and His hands were dripping with blood. It was too tragic to behold, and my heart was deeply wounded.”

In this broken yet sacred moment, he knelt beneath the cross in humble prayer until the midnight bell rang. When the midnight bell sounded, he heard the comforting voice of the Lord saying: “My child, your sins are forgiven!” This moment became the decisive turning point of his life, which he later called his “spiritual birthday.”

From that time on, he was given a new name, “John,” like John the Baptist, as a sign to bear witness to Christ’s return and His eschatological message to the nations. His burden was lifted, his heart was renewed, and he received a clear calling to proclaim the message of Christ’s return. This moment completely changed the direction of his life and ministry.

Immediately after his rebirth, a stranger handed him a globe. He later described this experience in highly symbolic language:

“I hurried back to my room and carefully examined the round sphere. Gradually, the spherical form transformed into a rectangle, resembling a long human figure carrying a large cross on its back. The head was labeled ‘Palestine,’ and the chest was labeled ‘China.’”

Although the symbolic meaning of this vision is not entirely clear, the spatial movement from “Palestine” to “China” can be understood as a fusion of the universal gospel with his personal missionary burden. It also expresses his deep conviction that China had been entrusted with participation in the way of the cross.

In his diary, John Sung described this experience as “rebirth”（再生，zai sheng） However, later scholarly studies have offered a more cautious interpretation. From a critical perspective, Daryl Ireland and Chris White suggest that Sung may have dramatized this experience to enhance its emotional and communicative impact, noting that he had already known the Lord during his time in the United States and had demonstrated a degree of religious zeal in his earlier life. Professor Lim Ka-Tong interprets this stage as a “rediscovery of faith,” arguing that after theological struggle and inner crisis, Sung returned to the gospel faith he had embraced earlier in life. Wang Tiansheng, on the other hand, offers a different theological interpretation, suggesting that this stage is better understood as a “being experience” in Sung’s spiritual life.

In an interview conducted on April 8, 2026, Wang Tiansheng explained that he divides Sung’s spiritual development into three stages: “knowing,” “doing,” and “being.” He further explains that salvation consists of two dimensions: first, a cognitive stage; and second, a relational stage, the latter being realized through union with Christ in crucifixion. This “being” stage corresponds to Sung’s inward experience of spiritual rebirth, in which his inner life underwent profound transformation. In Wang’s view, “rebirth” can be distinguished in two senses: one as an external, ritual expression of regeneration, and the other as the baptism of the Holy Spirit. What Sung experienced was not limited to the former, but rather belonged to the latter—an inward process in which his life was renewed and reshaped by the Holy Spirit.

In sum, this stage represents not merely a recovery of faith or a psychological turning point, but a process of dismantling and reconstruction of the self before the cross of Christ—a radical reconfiguration of life, identity, and calling in Christ. On this day, Sung wrote on the cover of his diary the words “華韓 (Hua Han, meaning ‘John of China’),” as a reminder of his calling (see Levi Sung, The Diary of John Sung, Genesis, 2012, p. 43).

——宋尚节的生命与事工

重生的经历

1926年最后一夜，宋尚节进入了一场极深的属灵危机，他的内心充满剧烈的挣扎与本体性认知的绝望。在祷告中，他听到神的警告：“我要灭绝智慧人的智慧”（林前1：19），这句话如同重锤击打在他心上，使他整个对人类知识、成就以及生命意义的信心根基都被动摇。

进入1927年中国农历新年之际，这种内在的重担并未减轻，反而愈发加重。到了1927年2月10日，即农历新年的第一天，他已无法再承受这沉重的属灵压迫。在这种危机状态中，他经历了圣灵与邪恶之间极其剧烈的内在争战，并对自己的罪性与完全无力产生了深刻的认识。

那一夜，在他后来所理解为圣灵光照的时刻，他的一生仿佛被完全摊开在神面前。他见证说，从童年到成年所有的罪，都如同电影般一幕幕在他眼前重现。他在极大的羞愧与绝望中，取出一本许久都未曾打开、放在书柜底部的圣经，并开始阅读路加福音第23章。

在《宋尚节博士见证》中，他描述这段经历时说：

“论到主耶稣为我罪受难的经过，我彷佛魂游象外，心神跟随背负十字架的耶稣到各各他，一路走去，荒凉寂静，这一幅活现着惨苦沉痛的图画，刺透了我的心。我自己也和去钉十架的罪人一般，沉下了头弯曲了背，眼都不敢斜视别人，只是蹒跚地踵着主的步履走。唉，朋友呀，那你可真不知道我那一刹时的难堪了！我一面跟主走上各各他的路，一面觉得背负着的重担不是我所能担当的，所负罪的重量几乎把我压死。不知怎的一来，耶稣已高悬在木架上了。头侧着，两手的鲜血淋漓。惨不忍睹，我心伤特甚”。

在这破碎而神圣的时刻，他跪在十字架下谦卑祷告，直到午夜钟声敲响。当午夜钟声敲响之时，他听见主安慰的声音说：“我的孩子，你的罪赦免了！”这一刻成为他生命决定性的转折点，他后来称之为自己的“属灵生日”。

从此，他被赋予一个新的名字“约翰”，仿佛施洗约翰一般，作为一个记号，要为基督再来与末世信息向万国作见证。他的重担被挪去，心灵被更新，并领受了清楚的呼召，要传扬基督再来的信息。这一刻彻底改变了他生命与事奉的方向。

就在他重生之后，一位陌生人交给他一个地球仪。他后来用极富象征性的语言描述这一经历：

“我急忙回到房间，仔细观看这个圆形球体。渐渐地，这球体变成一个长方形，像一个长形的人背负着一架大十字架。头部标记为‘巴勒斯坦’，胸部标记为‘中国’。”

虽然这一异象的象征意义并不完全清晰，但从“巴勒斯坦”到“中国”的空间转移，常被理解为福音的普世性与他个人宣教负担的结合，也表达了他深深的心志：他认为中国被托付参与十字架道路的使命。

在他的日记中，宋尚节将这一经历称为“再生”。然而，后来的学术研究对此做出更为审慎的理解。Daryl Ireland 与 Chris White 从批判角度指出，宋尚节可能对这一经历有所戏剧化，以增强其情感表达与传播效果，并指出他其实在赴美期间早已认识主，并在生命早期就具有一定的宗教热忱。林加东（Lim Ka-Tong）教授将这一阶段解释为“信仰的再发现”，认为他在经历神学冲击与内在危机之后，回到了早年所接受的福音信仰。王天成（Wang Tiansheng）则提出不同的神学诠释，认为这一阶段更适合被理解为其属灵生命中的一种“存有性经验”（being experience）。

在2026年4月8日王天生（Wang Tiansheng）在接受笔者采访时分享到：他将宋尚节的属灵发展划分为三个阶段：“知”（knowing）、“行”（doing）与“存有”（being）。他进一步解释救恩包含两个层面：首先是认知性的阶段，其次是关系的阶段，而后者是在与基督同钉十字架的联合中得以实现的。这一“存有”阶段，对应于宋尚节在内在经历中的属灵重生体验，在此过程中，他的内在生命经历了深刻的转化。在王天生看来，“重生”可以从两个意义上加以区分：其一是作为外在、礼仪性表达的重生；其二是圣灵的洗。宋尚节所经历的，并不仅限于前者，而是后者——即一个内在的过程，在其中他的生命被圣灵更新并重新塑造。

总的来说，这一阶段不仅仅是信仰的恢复，也不仅仅是心理性的转折，而是一个在基督十字架面前“自我被拆毁并重建”的过程——生命、身份与呼召在基督里被彻底重新塑造。这一天宋尚节在日记封面写下“華韓（Hua Han，意为‘中国的约翰’）”，作为对自己呼召的提醒（见Levi Sung《宋尚节日记》，Genesis出版社，2012年，第43页）。
