### A CONTEXTUALIZED EMBODIMENT OF THE CROSS

JOHN SUNG’S LIFE AND MINISTRY

(continued from above)

Crisis and Transformation: Studies in the United States

The Apostle Paul, reflecting on his younger years, once said that he had advanced in Judaism beyond many of his contemporaries and was extremely zealous for the traditions of his fathers (Gal. 1:14). In much the same way, John Sung, from an early age, possessed a strong ambition and an intense drive for achievement.

He grew up during a time when China was shaken by turmoil and national crisis. Many young people of that generation were inspired by the ideal of “saving the nation through education,” believing that knowledge could not only transform personal destiny, but also strengthen and rescue the nation. John Sung was exceptionally intelligent and diligent from childhood. With outstanding academic performance, he advanced step by step through higher education and eventually went to the United States for further study.

At first, he intended to enter theological studies in preparation for future service in the church and missionary work. However, after arriving in America, he gradually came to believe that theology could perhaps be studied independently, while science seemed to be the true path to strengthening the nation. As a result, he turned his attention to natural sciences and quickly demonstrated remarkable intellectual ability and competitiveness.

In only three years, at Ohio Wesleyan University, he completed his university studies and later earned both a master’s degree (1924) and a Ph.D. (1926) in chemistry with excellent academic distinction. In an era dominated by the ideal of “science saving the nation,” a Chinese preacher holding a doctorate in chemistry naturally attracted widespread attention. Later, many people were willing to attend his meetings simply because they heard that “a doctor of chemistry was preaching the gospel.”

From the world’s perspective, Sung at that time was undoubtedly successful: academically accomplished, intellectually active, socially engaged, and full of promise. He even organized fellowship gatherings between Black and White students, something highly unusual in America during that period, and these activities drew public and media attention. Yet behind all the excitement and outward achievement, his inner spiritual life remained deeply unsatisfied.

Looking back on those years later in life, Sung rebuked himself with great sorrow, saying that he had “trampled underfoot the precious blood of Christ” and had “despised the salvation of the cross.”

For at that time, he increasingly viewed Christianity as a moral ideal for “serving society” and “improving human life,” rather than the gospel of sinners being saved and reconciled to God. Jesus gradually became, in his eyes, merely a great moral teacher, rather than the Savior who died on the cross for sinners.

Such ideas might provide temporary comfort, cultural refinement, or even social improvement; yet they could never truly solve the deeper problems within the human soul — the problem of sin, the emptiness of the heart, and humanity’s separation from God.

At the same time, his academic success failed to bring genuine spiritual peace. Instead, under the influence of rationalism and liberal theology, his once-simple faith gradually began to weaken. This influence became especially pronounced during his studies at Union Theological Seminary in New York.

Later, he recalled that the methods of preaching and biblical interpretation he learned there could be summarized in this way:

“When philosophy could not explain the Bible, science was used to validate it.

If science could not verify it, then it was treated merely as symbolic language.

Whatever human reason could not fully comprehend was easily dismissed.

People would loudly declare, ‘This is unreasonable!’ or ‘This is unbelievable!’

And then, lightly and casually, they would cast aside the Word of God.”

Sung later came to realize deeply that when human reason is placed upon the throne, the Word of God will sooner or later be laid aside. Therefore, he warned believers to exercise spiritual discernment toward teachings that “sound profound and persuasive.” For some ideas may appear reasonable on the surface yet gradually lead people away from the cross.

During that season of confusion, he even became involved in Buddhist meditation practices. He also translated the Dao De Jing, the Daoism classical book, and at one point believed that “all religions are simply different roads leading to the same destination.”

Yet precisely in the midst of his deepest confusion and spiritual wandering, God was quietly preparing a profound work within him.

Very often, a person must first come to the end of himself before he can truly hunger for the cross.

Perhaps God allows people to experience the heights of knowledge, the glory of the world, and the pride of human reason, so that in the end they may finally understand:

What truly saves a person is not human wisdom, nor the philosophies of this world, but Jesus Christ and Him crucified.                                                          (To be continued)

一、危机与转变：在美国留学时期

使徒保罗回顾自己年轻时，曾说他在犹太教中比许多同龄人更有长进，并且为祖宗的传统大发热心（加1:14）。宋尚节年轻时，也带着同样强烈的追求与进取心。

他成长的时代，正是中国风雨飘摇、民族危机深重的时候。那一代许多青年人心中都怀着“读书救国”的理想，相信知识不仅能改变个人命运，也能振兴国家、拯救民族。宋尚节从小聪明勤奋，成绩十分优异，因此一路进入更高层次的教育体系，最终远赴美国留学。

起初，他原本是为了将来服事教会与宣教工作，准备进入神学领域。然而到了美国以后，他渐渐觉得：神学或许可以自己读书研究，而真正能够使国家富强的似乎是科学。于是，他开始转向自然科学，并且很快展现出惊人的学习能力和竞争力。

他在俄亥俄卫斯理大学（Ohio Wesleyan University）只用了三年时间，就完成了大学课程，后来又取得化学硕士（1924）与博士学位（1926），而且成绩都十分优秀。在那个“科学救国”的年代，一位拥有化学博士学位的中国传道人，自然格外引人注目。后来，许多人也是因为听说“有一位化学博士在讲道”，才愿意走进聚会听福音。

从世人的眼光来看，那时的宋尚节无疑是成功的：学业优秀、前途光明、思想活跃，也积极参与各种社会活动。他甚至组织黑人与白人学生之间的团契聚会，在当时的美国，这是十分少见的事情，也因此受到媒体关注。然而，在这一切热闹与成就的背后，他里面的灵命却并没有真正满足。

后来，宋尚节回想那段日子时，非常痛心地责备自己，说自己曾“践踏基督宝血”、“轻看十字架的救恩”。

因为那时，他越来越把信仰理解成一种“服务社会”、“改善人生”的道德理想，而不是罪人得救、人与神和好的福音。耶稣渐渐被看成一位伟大的道德家，却不再是那位为罪人钉十字架的救主。

这样的思想，也许能够给人暂时的安慰、文化的提升，甚至社会的改善；但却无法真正解决人里面罪的问题、空虚的问题、与神隔绝的问题。与此同时，他在学术上的成功，也没有给他带来真正属灵的平安。相反，在理性主义与自由派神学的影响下，他原本单纯的信心开始渐渐动摇。尤其是在纽约协和神学院学习期间，这种影响更加明显。

他后来回忆说，当时所学到的讲道与解经方法，大致是这样的：

“当哲学无法解释圣经时，就用科学来证明；若科学无法证明，就说那只是象征性的说法；凡是人的理性无法完全理解的，就被轻易地否定。于是，人常常高声说：‘这不合理！’、‘这不可信！’ 然后便轻轻忽忽地把神的话丢在一边。

宋尚节后来深深意识到：若人的理性坐上宝座，神的话语迟早会被人放下。因此，他提醒信徒，对于那些“听起来很高深、很动人”的思想，需要有属灵的分辨。因为有些道理表面上似乎很合理，却会慢慢使人的心离开十字架。

在那段迷茫时期，他甚至开始接触佛教禅修，也尝试翻译《道德经》，并一度认为：“各种宗教不过都是通往同一个目标的不同道路。” 然而，就在他里面最混乱、最迷失的时候，神却正在暗暗预备一次极深的属灵工作。

很多时候，人必须先走到自己的尽头，才会真正渴慕十字架。

也许神允许人经历知识的高峰、世界的荣耀、理性的骄傲，为的是让人最终明白：

真正能够拯救人的，不是人的聪明，不是世界的哲学，而是耶稣基督并他钉十字架。
