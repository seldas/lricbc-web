Spiritual Roots: Childhood and Youth

Sung Xuelian (c. 1863–1918), the father of John Sung, served as the fourteenth pastor of the Putian Central Church.[17] Beyond his pastoral responsibilities, he held multiple leadership roles, including district superintendent, school principal, editor of The Revivalist (奮興報, pinyin: Fenxing Bao),[18] and conference evangelist.[19]

In due course, after both had experienced spiritual rebirth, they welcomed their sixth child, John Sung (Chinese name: Shangjie 尚节, meaning “to esteem moral integrity and self-discipline”), whose childhood name was “Tian’en” (“Heavenly Grace”).[24]

As a Chinese saying goes, “The soil of a region shapes its people.” The rural environment of childhood has profoundly shaped the preaching styles of many renowned preachers. For example, Charles H. Spurgeon viewed nature as a vital source of instruction for preaching. He remarked that a flower, a bird, an insect, and a fish became his most outstanding teachers. He further observed:   “Sermons glisten in the morning on every blade of grass, and homilies fly by you as the sere leaves fall from the trees. A forest is a library, a cornfield is a volume of philosophy, the rock is a history, and the river at its base a poem… Books are poor things compared with these.” [25] Reflecting on his hometown, John Sung proudly stated that Fengji Village, his hometown, was surrounded by mountains.

“The scenery is elegant and the land is fertile. God placed me from childhood in a place

of green mountains and clear waters, where flowers are fragrant and birds sing, to shape

and refine me. I lived in the embrace of nature and came to know God, who created

heaven and earth and governs all things. Ah, the God of abundant grace toward me

reveals His wondrous works everywhere.”[26]

His father took him to the mountain where he preached and prayed for believers.[27] Moreover, at the age of twelve, he earned the title “Little Pastor” by preaching on behalf of his father.[28]

Martin Luther’s decision to enter the monastery was precipitated by the famous thunderstorm vow of 1505,[29] though it likely occurred within a broader context of existential anxiety, including encounter with sudden death among his peer.[30] In a similar way, John Sung experienced several decisive events that profoundly shaped his early spiritual awareness. Philip Koo identifies three formative influences on his childhood spirituality. First, the death of his younger sister prompted him to reflect deeply on the questions of death and eternal life. Second, the Xinghua Revival,[31] provided a powerful revivalistic context that further influenced his spiritual development. Third, in 1909, he witnessed a remarkable answer to prayer when he interceded for his father, whose severe asthmatic condition was dramatically healed—an experience that left a lasting impression on him regarding the efficacy of prayer.[32]

属灵萌芽：童年与少年时期

宋尚节的父亲宋学廉（约1863–1918）曾任莆田中央堂第十四任牧师。[1] 除了牧养教会之外，他还身兼多项事工领袖角色，包括区会监督、学校校长、《奋兴报》[2]主编，以及大会布道大会讲员。[3]

宋尚节讲到他父亲自称“虽不是有名的骚客墨卿，可确是个书香子弟，家有文房四宝”。[4] 宋学廉在十八岁，即神学学习的最后一年，经历了重生。当他研读《约翰福音》与《罗马书》时，圣灵光照他，使他为自己的罪深感扎心，进而在神面前一项一项地认罪，并得着赦罪的平安。[5] 这一经历成为他信仰生命中重要的转折点。此后，当他在事奉中遭遇艰难、甚至萌生退意时，神的话临到他说：：“我的仆人啊！不要怕，有我！你所需用的，我早已都知道了。”[6]

宋尚节的母亲陈若兰在一场重病得医治之后，也经历了属灵的重生，并甘心与丈夫一同走十字架的道路。[7]此后，在他们双双经历重生之后，他们迎来了第六个孩子——宋尚节（“尚节”意为“崇尚节操、自守品格”），乳名“天恩”。[8]

正如中国俗语所言：“一方水土养育一方人。”童年的乡村环境深刻地塑造了许多著名讲员的讲道风格。例如，司布真（Charles H. Spurgeon）将大自然视为讲道的重要源泉。他曾指出，一朵花、一只鸟、一只昆虫、一条鱼，都成为他最卓越的老师。他又说：“清晨，每一片草叶上都闪耀着讲章；当枯叶从树上飘落时，讲道的信息也在你身旁飞舞。森林是一座图书馆，玉米田是一卷哲学之书，岩石是一部历史，而其下流淌的河水则是一首诗……与这些相比，书本实在显得贫乏。”[9] 回忆自己的故乡时，宋尚节满怀深情地说，自己的家乡凤迹群山环绕：  “风景雅致，水土宜人。上帝就把我从小安置在山青水秀，花香鸟语的胜地，陶我冶我。我侧身在大自然的怀抱中，认识了创造天地、宰治万物的上帝。啊！厚恩待我的上帝，随在显见祂奇妙的作为。”[10] 他的父亲常带他上山，在那里讲道，并为信徒祷告。[11] 不仅如此，他在十二岁时便开始代替父亲讲道，因此得了“小牧师”的称号。[12]

马丁·路德（Martin Luther）之所以决定进入修道院，通常被归因于1505年那次著名的雷暴誓愿，[13] 尽管这一决定很可能也发生在更广泛的存在性焦虑背景之中，包括他曾目睹同伴突然去世的经历。[14] 类似地，宋尚节在年少时期也经历了一系列关键事件，深刻塑造了他的属灵意识。Philip Koo 指出，有三方面因素对他童年的属灵生命产生了重要影响：第一，幼妹的去世促使他深刻思考死亡与永生的问题；第二，兴化大复兴[15]为他提供了一个强烈的复兴性属灵环境；第三，1909年他曾为患严重哮喘的父亲迫切代祷，并亲眼见证神奇妙的医治，这一经历在他心中留下了深刻印象，使他切实认识到祷告的大能。[16]

John Sung described his father, Xuelian, as a Chinese scholar from a traditional literati household where the “Four Treasures of the Study” (文房四寶) formed part of the cultural environment. John Sung recorded that his father remarked, “Although I am not a renowned man of letters, I am indeed a son of a scholarly household, with the Four Treasures of the Study(文房四寶) in my home.” [20] Xuelian was born again at the age of eighteen, during his final year of seminary studies. While reading the books of John and Romans, he experienced the illumination of the Holy Spirit, which convicted him of his sins, led him to confess them before God, and brought him the peace of forgiveness.[21] This marked a decisive watershed in his faith. When he encountered hardships in his ministry and considered giving up, he experienced a strong sense of divine assurance, “My servant, do not be afraid; I am with you. What you need, I have already known.”[22] After his mother, Chen Ruolan, recovered from a serious illness and experienced spiritual rebirth, she willingly chose to walk the way of the cross alongside her husband.[23]
